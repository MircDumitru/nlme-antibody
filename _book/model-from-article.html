The non-linear model proposed in (Pasin et al. 2019) based on three compartments, i.e. the SL cells (S), the LL cells (L) and the antibodies (A) writes

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \frac{\text{d}S}{\text{d}t} & = - \delta_S S , \\ \frac{\text{d}L}{\text{d}t} & = - \delta_L L , \\ \frac{\text{d}A}{\text{d}t} & = \theta_S S + \theta_L L - \delta_S A , \end{split} \end{equation} \tag{4.1}

with

  • \delta_S representing the decay rate over time of the long-lived (SL) antibody-secreting cells,

  • \theta_S representing the antibodies production rate of SL antibody-secreting cells,

  • \delta_L representing the decay rate over time of the long-lived (LL) antibodies,

  • \theta_L representing the antibodies production rate of LL antibody-secreting cells,

  • \delta_A representing the decay rate of antibodies.

The equation for the antibodies A is the non-homogeneous linear ordinary differential equation (ODE) given by:

\begin{equation} \frac{\text{d}A}{\text{d}t} = \phi_S \exp{\left(-\delta_S t \right)} + \phi_L \exp{\left( -\delta_L t \right)} - \delta_A A , \end{equation} \tag{4.2}

with

\begin{equation} \phi_S = \theta_S S_0, \quad S_0 = S(0) , \end{equation} \tag{4.3}

and \begin{equation} \phi_L = \theta_L L_0, \quad L_0 = L(0) , \end{equation} \tag{4.4}

Equation 4.2 writes

\begin{equation} \frac{\text{d}A}{\text{d}t} + \delta_A A = \phi_S \exp(-\delta_S t) + \phi_L \exp(-\delta_L t) \end{equation} \tag{4.5}

or equivalently

\begin{equation} \frac{\text{d}A}{\text{d}t} + P(t) A = Q(t) \end{equation} \tag{4.6}

with:

  • P(t) = \delta_f (i.e. constant),
  • Q(t) = \phi_S \exp(-\delta_S t) + \phi_L \exp(-\delta_L t).

The homogeneous equation writes

\begin{equation} \frac{\text{d}A}{\text{d}t} + \delta_A A = 0 , \end{equation} \tag{4.7}

with the solution

\begin{equation} A_h(t) = c \exp(-\delta_A t) , \end{equation} \tag{4.8}

with c a constant determined by the initial conditions (i.e A(0)). The non-homogeneous part can be solved using an integrating factor. Let the integrating factor be

\begin{equation} \mu(t) = \exp{ \left( \int \delta_A \text{d}t \right) } = \exp{(\delta_A t)} , \end{equation} \tag{4.9}

and multiplying by the integrating factor, Equation 4.5 writes

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \frac{\text{d} }{\text{d}t} \left( \exp(\delta_f t) f(t) \right) & = \phi_S \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_S) t \right) \\ & + \phi_L \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_L) t \right) . \end{split} \end{equation} \tag{4.10}

Integrating both sides (with respect to t) writes:

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \exp(\delta_A t) f(t) & = \phi_S \int \exp{ \left( (\delta_A - \delta_S) t \right) } \text{d}t \\ & + \phi_L \int \exp{ \left( (\delta_A - \delta_L) t \right) } \text{d}t . \end{split} \end{equation} \tag{4.11}

The first integral writes

\begin{equation} \int \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_S) t \right) \text{d}t = \frac{1}{\delta_A - \delta_S} \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_S) t \right) + c_1 , \end{equation} \tag{4.12}

and the second integral writes

\begin{equation} \int \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_L) t \right) \text{d}t = \frac{1}{\delta_A - \delta_L} \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_L) t \right) + c_2 . \end{equation} \tag{4.13}

Plugging Equation 4.12 and Equation 4.13 in Equation 4.11 writes

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \exp(\delta_A t) f(t) & = \frac{\phi_S}{\delta_A - \delta_S} \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_S) t \right) \\ & + \frac{\phi_L}{\delta_A - \delta_L} \exp \left( (\delta_A - \delta_L) t \right) \\ & + c . \end{split} \end{equation} \tag{4.14}

hence

\begin{equation} \begin{split} A(t) & = c \exp(-\delta_A t) \\ & + \frac{\phi_S}{\delta_A - \delta_S} \exp \left( -\delta_S t \right) \\ & + \frac{\phi_L}{\delta_A - \delta_L} \exp \left( -\delta_L t \right) , \end{split} \end{equation} \tag{4.15}

with c a constant determined by the initial condition (i.e. A(0)) plugged-in from the data, i.e. the abelisa value corresponding to the second time instance measurement (i.e. the 7 days corresponding measurement). Assuming the second time instance measurement is A(0) = a, the constant writes

\begin{equation} c = a - \frac{\phi_S}{\delta_A - \delta_S} - \frac{\phi_L}{\delta_A - \delta_L} . \end{equation} \tag{4.16}

Let \bm{\psi} denote the vector of parameters for the decay rates (LL antibodies, SL antibodies and antibodies) and \phi_S and \phi_L

\begin{equation} \bm{\psi}= \begin{bmatrix} \phi_S & \delta_S & \phi_L & \delta_L & \delta_{Ab} \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation} \tag{4.17}

The following mixed-effect model each parameter \xi_{k}, with k = 1, \ldots, 5 is considered:

\begin{equation} \log{\left( \xi_{k}^{i} \right)} = \log{\left( \xi_{k0} \right)} + \bm{\beta}^{T}_{k} \mathbf{z}_{k}^{i} + \nu_{k}^{i} , \end{equation} \tag{4.18}

where

  • \xi^{i}_{k} represents the kth parameters corresponding to the ith subject,

  • \xi_{k0} represents the fixed effect corresponding to the kth element of the parameters vector,

  • \mathbf{z}^{i}_{k} represents the vector of independent variables of the model,

  • \bm{\beta}_{k} represents the coefficents corresponding to \mathrm{z}^{i}_{k},

  • \nu^{i}_{k} represents the individual effect, assumed to

    • follow a centered normal distributed with variance \omega_{k}^{2}

    \begin{equation} \nu^{i}_{k} \sim \mathcal N \left( 0, \omega_{k}^{2} \right) \end{equation} \tag{4.19}

    • be mutually independent

    \begin{equation} \nu^{i}_{k} \perp \!\!\!\! \perp\nu^{j}_{l} \end{equation} \tag{4.20}

The antibody concentration for patient i at the jth time instance measurement is assumed to be an additional error model

\begin{equation} \mathrm{y}_{ij} = \log_{10} \left( A(\bm{\psi}^{i},t_{ij}) \right) + \epsilon_{ij} , \end{equation} \tag{4.21}

with the noise following a centered normal distributed with the same variance \omega_{A}^{2} (homoscedasticity), i.e.

\begin{equation} \epsilon_{ij} \sim \mathcal N \left( 0, \sigma_{A}^{2} \right) . \end{equation} \tag{4.22}

The complete set of model’s parameters is

\begin{equation} \bm{\theta} = \begin{bmatrix} \left( \xi_{k0} \right)_{k = 1, 5} & \left( \bm{\beta}_{k} \right)_{k = 1, 5} & \left( \omega_{k} \right)_{k = 1, 5} & \sigma_{A} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation} \tag{4.23}
